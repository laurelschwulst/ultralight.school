Overview

This is the class homepage for Research as a Leisure Activity, co-taught by Celine Nguyen & Laurel Schwulst, for Ultralight School.

In the form of a “convivial weekend retreat,” this class is about how research and appreciation can help us create new works. We’ll collect things that inspire us — let’s call them our “shimmering points” — share them with each other, and draw from them when creating something new. These can be new works of art, writing, or whatever medium you feel most expressive in. We’ll begin on Friday evening and finish on Sunday afternoon. You’ll have both quiet working time and more sociable, conversational time to learn from other participants. We’ll also include collaborative exercises — more like conversations than conventional critiques — where we’ll help shepherd each other’s projects into being.

About this site

This is the internal class website for the participants of Ultralight School's "Research as a Leisure Activity" class. It is intended for anyone who is taking the class. It was created by Laurel Schwulst and has the background color of greenlight or hex code #eafede.

Confidentiality & Conduct

This site is intended as a class "hub" for the weekend's activities. Please do not share it or its contents outside of this class without our explicit permission. Thank you.

We hope to form a supportive and mutually curious cohort; see our code of conduct for more details. We’re trying to understand — and help each other understand — what we’re drawn to, and how that might shape our own practices. This will involve a good deal of collecting and introspecting and reflecting. But we should be lighthearted about it! Especially with the desired output of the class. Research as leisure activity is also a lifelong activity, and what we do this weekend is just one part of the overall creative lives we might be living.
