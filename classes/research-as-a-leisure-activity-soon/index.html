Research as Leisure Activity is a class (in the form of a convivial weekend retreat) about how research and appreciation can help us create new works. We’ll collect things that inspire us — let’s call them our references, or “shimmering points” — share them with each other, and draw from them when creating something new. These can be new works of art, writing, or whatever medium(s) you feel most expressive in.

This class is taught by Celine Nguyen & Laurel Schwulst and hosted in-person in New York City. We’ll begin on Friday evening and finish on Sunday afternoon. You’ll have both quiet working time and more sociable, conversational time to learn from other participants. We’ll also include collaborative exercises — more like conversations than conventional critiques — where we’ll help shepherd each other’s projects into being.

We hope that you’ll emerge from the weekend with:

* A collection of shimmering points that have shaped your interests

* A refined understanding of how to harness them in your own work (and by “work,” we mean anything — your writing, your visual art, your sculptures, your websites, your community endeavors)

* A finished work

There’s a special quality that comes from starting projects, and a different, also special quality that comes from finishing them. We’ve found that retreats, classes, and residencies can offer us protected space to reflect on our practices, and push us in new, exciting directions afterwards. We’d like this class to be a container that helps you start and finish something in a weekend. It might even shape the work that you’ll do later on this summer, autumn, and beyond.
