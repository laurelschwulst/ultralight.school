Overview

This is the class homepage for Sense to Sense, co-taught by Meg Miller & Laurel Schwulst, for Ultralight School.

This is a publishing class about translation and the senses. Over five weeks, we will engage in readings and discussion about the five senses and explore what it means to carry sensations over into written form. How can a fragrance become a poem? What are the different ways to write an image, or to document a sound? Similarly, we’ll look toward the senses to expand our use of language and to strengthen our writing voices. By the end of class, we’ll collect and publish some of our experiments into a publication.

Structure

The class meets for ten sessions over the course of five weeks.

Sessions happen on Sunday evenings (7-8pm) virtually for reading discussion, and Monday evenings (6-9pm) in-person (at 323 Dean Street #1 in Boerum Hill, Brooklyn, NY) for exercises, sharing work, and publication making.

Specific dates: 6.15, 6.16, 6.22, 6.23, 6.29, 6.30, 7.6, 7.7, 7.13, 7.14 — subscribe to our class Google Calendar!

Readings

You can always find the readings for the discussions on Sundays on this site and in our “Readings and References” Are.na channel.

Please read actively, marking passages, writing down quotes and reflections, and recording questions as they arise. In class, we’ll be discussing the ideas and concepts that resonate with us, but also anything that moved us on the line-level: special attention to composition, turns of phrases, passages you found beautiful/awful/intriguing. All commentary is welcome; our hope is that being in dialogue with each other will help us understand the readings better, deeper, and/or differently.
