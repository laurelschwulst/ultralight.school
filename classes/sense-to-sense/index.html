Sense to Sense is a publishing workshop about translation and the senses. Over five weeks, participants will engage in readings and discussion about the five senses and explore what it means to carry sensations over into written form. How can a fragrance become a poem? What are the different ways to write an image, or to document a sound? If as Ursula Le Guin says, “all writing is translation,” from the perceptual into language, we’ll look toward the senses to expand our use of language, to break us out of writing ruts, and to strengthen our writing voice. By the end of class, we’ll collect and publish some of our writing experiments in a publication. If we’re feeling adventurous, we may also translation the publication into different modalities and mediums — audio, video, web, etc…

This class happens two consecutive days of the week (Sunday & Monday) for five weeks. We’ll meet: 1) Virtually on Sundays for one hour reading discussions, 2) In-person in Brooklyn, NY on Mondays for three hours of exercises, sharing work, and publication making.
