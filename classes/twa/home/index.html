Overview

This is the class homepage for TWA: Total Work of Art, co-taught by Bryant Wells, Stephen Kwok, & Laurel Schwulst, for Ultralight School.

In “TWA: Total Work of Art” we invite artists, designers, writers, programmers, and anyone else interested to shapeshift their concept across three modalities: 1) digital, 2) physical, and 3) temporal. Inspired by wide-ranging examples of design as world-building — think Bauhaus, Beyoncé, Bernadette Corporation — this class responds to how culture is experienced today, inviting participants to develop a core concept across multiple points and moments of access.

Structure

The class meets for thirteen sessions over the course of seven weeks.

Sessions happen on Sunday afternoons (noon-4pm) and Thursday evenings (7-9pm) in part of June, all of July, and the very beginning of August. We meet in-person at Telos (303 Ten Eyck Street) in East Williamsburg, Brooklyn, New York.

Specific dates: 6.22 (Orientation), 6.26, 6.28, 7.3, 7.6, 7.10, 7.13, 7.17, 7.20, 7.24, 7.27, 7.31, 8.3 (Finale). You can also explore our class Google Calendar — Subscribe, View.

twa project descriptions

Part 0. Point of Departure
Establish the core concept of the work you would like to do over the course of this workshop – your “point of departure” defines the central ideas that will shapeshift throughout the course of this workshop workshop.

Part 1. Digital Encounter
Translate your idea into a ‘digital encounter’. Defined by the ability to be circulated (electronically) your digital encounter may manifest as a website, an app, a newsletter, an e-book, etc.

Part 2. Physical Encounter
Translate your idea into a ‘physical encounter’. Defined by the ability to take up space, your physical encounter may manifest as a book, an object, a garment, a sculpture, an installation, etc.

Part 3. Temporal Encounter
Finally, translate your idea into a ‘Temporal Encounter’. Defined by the ability to start and end, a temporal encounter may manifest as a ceremony, a screening, a concert, a song, a dinner, a reading, a happening, etc.
